Literature Context

This peptide is the famous pp65495-503 epitope from human cytomegalovirus, one of the most studied CD8+ T cell epitopes in immunology with 754 documented T cell assays showing 97.7% positive response rate. The cited papers demonstrate its use as a model antigen for studying T cell responses, with documented public TCR clonotypes shared across HLA-A*02:01+ individuals. The epitope has been extensively validated in clinical contexts including CMV monitoring in transplant patients and as a positive control in immunomonitoring assays. Its presence in cancer vaccine analysis suggests either contamination, use as a positive control, or consideration as a benchmark comparator rather than a tumor neoantigen.

Mechanistic Reasoning

This peptide derives from the CMV pp65 tegument protein and elicits memory T cell responses in >70% of HLA-A*02:01+ individuals due to widespread CMV seropositivity. The high immunogenicity stems from viral origin, avoiding central tolerance mechanisms that limit responses to self-antigens. For cancer vaccination, this represents a non-tumor antigen that would not target cancer cells unless engineered as a surrogate marker or unless the tumor expresses CMV antigens. The robust pre-existing memory response in CMV+ patients could theoretically be redirected if tumor cells were engineered to present this epitope.
